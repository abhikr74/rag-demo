
# ACME CORPORATION IT SECURITY POLICY

## Document Control
- Version: 2.1
- Effective Date: January 1, 2024
- Review Date: January 1, 2025
- Owner: IT Security Team

## 1. Introduction

This document outlines the IT security policies and procedures that all ACME Corporation employees must follow to protect company information systems and data.

## 2. Access Control

### 2.1 User Account Management
- All user accounts must be approved by department managers
- Accounts for terminated employees must be disabled within 24 hours
- Regular access reviews are conducted quarterly

### 2.2 Password Requirements
- Minimum 12 characters in length
- Must include uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and special characters
- Cannot reuse last 12 passwords
- Must be changed every 90 days
- Multi-factor authentication required for all systems

### 2.3 Password Reset Procedure
If you forget your password:
1. Go to the company intranet password reset page
2. Enter your employee ID and email address
3. Check your email for reset instructions
4. Follow the link and create a new password
5. If issues persist, contact IT support at ext. 4357

## 3. Data Classification and Handling

### 3.1 Data Classification Levels
- **Public**: Information that can be freely shared
- **Internal**: Information for internal use only
- **Confidential**: Sensitive business information
- **Restricted**: Highly sensitive information requiring special handling

### 3.2 Data Storage Requirements
- All company data must be stored on approved systems
- Confidential data must be encrypted at rest and in transit
- Regular backups are performed nightly
- Data retention follows company policy (7 years for financial data)

## 4. Incident Response

### 4.1 Security Incident Reporting
If you suspect a security incident:
1. Immediately report to IT Security at security@acme.com
2. Do not attempt to investigate or resolve yourself
3. Preserve evidence by not modifying affected systems
4. Document what you observed

### 4.2 Incident Response Team
- IT Security Manager: John Smith (ext. 4400)
- Network Administrator: Sarah Johnson (ext. 4401)
- System Administrator: Mike Brown (ext. 4402)

## 5. Acceptable Use

### 5.1 Computer and Network Usage
- Company systems are for business use primarily
- Limited personal use is permitted during breaks
- Prohibited activities include gambling, illegal downloads, and accessing inappropriate content
- All usage is monitored and logged

### 5.2 Email and Communication
- Professional communication standards apply
- Do not forward chain letters or spam
- Be cautious with attachments from unknown sources
- Report suspicious emails to security@acme.com

## 6. Mobile Device Policy

### 6.1 Company-Provided Devices
- Must be protected with device passcode/biometric lock
- Automatic screen lock after 5 minutes of inactivity
- Company has right to remote wipe if device is lost/stolen

### 6.2 Personal Devices (BYOD)
- Must install company security app before accessing company email
- Regular security scans required
- Separate work profile for company data

## 7. Remote Work Security

### 7.1 Home Network Requirements
- Secure Wi-Fi with WPA3 encryption
- Regular router firmware updates
- No shared/public Wi-Fi for company work

### 7.2 VPN Usage
- Always connect through company VPN when working remotely
- VPN must be active for all company system access
- Report VPN connection issues immediately

## 8. Training and Awareness

### 8.1 Security Training
- Annual security awareness training mandatory for all employees
- Phishing simulation tests conducted quarterly
- Additional training required for high-risk roles

### 8.2 Policy Updates
- Employees notified of policy changes via email
- Annual acknowledgment of policy understanding required
- Questions should be directed to security@acme.com
